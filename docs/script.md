# Voiceover Script

Runtime target: about 6 minutes 40 seconds at 135-145 words per minute.

## 0:00-0:35 Hook

Ghost and Zoro are entering the Grand Line of trading card investing.

Not as tourists. Not as hype chasers. As a two-person crew trying to turn a young One Piece card position into a disciplined portfolio.

The treasure is not one card. It is a repeatable system: buy into real demand, protect the downside, sell into hype, and hold the legends.

One Piece has story power. Pokemon has liquidity power. The opportunity is understanding where those two worlds overlap, and where they do not.

## 0:35-1:25 One Piece TCG Context

One Piece began as manga in 1997 and became an anime in 1999. The modern Bandai One Piece Card Game launched in 2022, after decades of character equity had already formed around Luffy, Zoro, Nami, Ace, Sabo, Roger, Shanks, and the wider world.

That matters. Collectors are not only buying cardboard mechanics. They are buying memory, identity, story, and status.

The card language matters too: set codes like OP, ST, and PR; rarity bands from common through secret rare; alternate art; event promos; pre-release stamps; anniversary campaign cards; and premium finishes. In One Piece, the best long-term cards usually combine three things: iconic character, hard-to-repeat source, and clean condition.

## 1:25-2:20 Market Comparison

Pokemon is the mature benchmark. It has deep liquidity, massive population data, and buyer confidence across raw cards, sealed product, and graded slabs.

One Piece is newer. That makes it more volatile, but also more interesting. Bandai Namco says Bandai ranked third in global TCG market share as of March 2025, and that growth was driven by the One Piece Card Game. PSA also reported a historic grading surge across the hobby, with more than 19 million cards graded in 2025 and another strong year-over-year jump in early 2026.

So the correct claim is careful: One Piece is not proven here to be bigger than Pokemon. It is a rising lane inside a much larger TCG boom.

## 2:20-3:25 Portfolio Analysis

Your current visible Collectr book is small, focused, and already thematic.

The anchor is Nami from the CS 2023 Celebration Pack at four hundred dollars. Then Zoro-Juurou at one hundred thirty-seven dollars and forty-nine cents. Franky, Luffy, Crocodile, another Luffy promo, and Carrot round out the One Piece side. The only clear off-thesis card is The Dark Magicians.

The Phygitals inbound book adds six graded items across two shipments, five of them One Piece. That is good. But it also means the next step is not more random buying. The next step is cataloging, comping, and deciding what each card is for: hold, flip, trade, or avoid.

On Courtyard, you have thirteen active offers totaling one hundred eighty-seven dollars and fifty cents. That is disciplined. The issue is quality control. Low-grade CGC slabs should only be bought at true discount prices. Iconic PSA and CGC 10 targets deserve more patience.

## 3:25-4:35 Strategy

The strategy is balanced flips plus holds.

Hold the cards that have identity: Luffy, Zoro, Nami, Ace, Sabo, Roger, Shanks, Teach, Newgate. Hold event context: pre-release, tournament pack, anniversary, treasure campaign, WANTED, Super Alt-Art, and Red Super Alt-Art.

Flip the cards that are liquid but replaceable. If you win a slab far below sold comps, do not fall in love with it. Move it into strength and recycle the cash.

Avoid weak low-grade slabs, especially when the character is not iconic and the card has no event source. Avoid paying PSA 10 pricing for CGC 9 liquidity. Avoid sealed product above MSRP unless sold comps prove the spread.

The goal is not to own the most cards. The goal is to own cards that other collectors will compete for.

## 4:35-5:35 Where To Buy And Sell

Courtyard and Phygitals are useful for slab sourcing and fast digital liquidity. TCGplayer is strong for North American raw and singles selling. eBay and PSA Vault are still important for graded slabs, but fees, shipping, and cross-border friction matter. Cardmarket gives European One Piece comps and can reveal Japanese-English price gaps.

In Cape Town, the key immediate event is Collect-a-Con Cape Town on June 6, 2026. Bring a short list, a price sheet, and a strict buy box. The Card Cache is a Cape Town-based store with One Piece singles, sealed products, and graded cards. TCGXchange and Wizards Books and Games are useful local verification points, especially for sealed pricing, accessories, and broader TCG demand.

For Facebook and Discord, use safeguards: public meetups, seller references, clear cert photos, no rushed payments, and no deals where the seller avoids verification.

## 5:35-6:30 Action Plan

For the first thirty days, receive the inbound slabs, photograph them, verify certs, and update the portfolio. Set hard buy ceilings: sixty-five to seventy-five percent of conservative sold comp for flips, up to ninety percent only for conviction holds.

In days thirty-one to sixty, sell or trade the off-thesis Yu-Gi-Oh slab if a fair buyer appears. Test one lower-conviction listing to learn your real sell-through speed. Build a watchlist around OP-13, EB-03, anniversary promos, and iconic character slabs.

In days sixty-one to ninety, rebalance toward sixty percent holds, twenty-five percent flips, and fifteen percent cash reserve. Cash is part of the portfolio because it lets you buy when other sellers need liquidity.

## 6:30-7:00 Closing

This is the operating thesis.

Build the crew. Protect the treasure. Sell into hype. Hold the legends.

Ghost and Zoro do not need to win every auction. They need to build a better map than the buyers around them.

That is how a small card position becomes a real collecting business.
