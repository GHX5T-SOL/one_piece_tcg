# OP CPT / The Vault Room Pricing Protocol v4

**Version:** v4
**Updated:** 2026-06-21
**Owner:** The Vault Room / OP CPT
**Primary use:** One Piece TCG, Pokemon TCG, Yu-Gi-Oh, Dragon Ball, graded slabs, sealed products, raw singles, consignment, acquisition bids, trade-show pricing, WhatsApp/Instagram pricing, website catalogue pricing, and pre-grading value analysis.

---

## 0. Non-Negotiable Standing Rule

Never price from Collectr alone.

Collectr is useful as a portfolio baseline and SKU discovery tool, but it is not a final pricing authority. Every serious price must be built from exact identity, live comps, current availability, grade/population context, condition, local Cape Town scarcity, channel fees, and The Vault Room's patient-selling posture.

If a tool, browser session, or market source can verify the answer, use it. If it cannot be verified, mark the field `needs_research` instead of guessing.

---

## 1. Business Context

The Vault Room is a Cape Town collector business and community for cards, collectibles, grails, trading, consigning, grading support, live shows, pack rips, and events.

Default posture:

- ZAR is the primary selling currency for local shows, WhatsApp, Instagram, Loyverse, and the website.
- USD remains the main evidence currency for eBay, PriceCharting, PSA, CGC, BGS, Card Ladder, TCGplayer, and many international comps.
- The Vault Room is not in a rush to sell scarce or appreciating inventory.
- Start high, negotiate down, and protect rare One Piece inventory.
- Local in-hand supply in Cape Town is often materially scarcer than global screenshots imply.
- A buyer at a show pays for instant inspection, trust, local handover, no international shipping delay, no customs surprise, no return dispute, and no proxy risk.
- Raw mint cards can carry grading-upside premium, but raw cards must never be sold as guaranteed PSA/BGS/CGC 10s.
- Customer-facing material may say "mint candidate", "pre-grade reviewed", or "strong grade candidate" only when supported by inspection notes.
- Customer-facing files must never expose cost basis, floor prices, staff notes, private owner/source labels, personal details, order details, wallet details, or negotiation strategy.

This protocol is practical business guidance, not financial advice and not a guarantee of future card prices.

## 2. Current Market Context To Consider

Use current research on every serious pricing run. As of this update:

- PSA announced a $200 million infrastructure investment on 2026-05-14 and reported heavy grading demand, long completion ranges for lower tiers, and pricing/service changes. This supports including grading backlog, grading cost, and time-lock risk in grading EV.
- PSA's current card grading page shows dynamic service levels, current prices, and turnaround estimates. Always check the live page before quoting grading cost.
- CGC's current card fees show lower entry tiers than PSA for many cards, with its own turnaround and declared-value limits. Always check the live page.
- CGC distinguishes Gem Mint 10 from Pristine 10. Do not price CGC Gem Mint 10 as CGC Pristine 10.
- BGS Black Label and BGS 10 Pristine are distinct from BGS 9.5 Gem Mint and should not be cross-priced casually.
- GemRate's Universal Search and grading recaps provide useful population and grading-volume context across PSA, Beckett, SGC, and CGC, but they still require exact identity checking.
- Card Ladder provides deep historical sales and population surfaces across public sales, but its values are not final sell prices by themselves.
- OP-16 The Time of Battle is now a live 2026 market catalyst: official English release was June 12, 2026, with OP-16 Release Event June 5-11, 2026. OP-16, Paramount War, Treasure Rare, SP, manga-like, major character, and event-pack items need recency-aware pricing.
- South African import VAT and customs friction can justify a local in-hand premium where the buyer avoids international ordering risk. Use SARS guidance rather than vague assumptions when explaining import-cost logic.

## 3. Exact Identification Comes First

Before quoting any serious buy, sell, bid, trade, grade, or consignment price, identify:

- Card or product name
- Set or product name
- Card number or product code
- Language and region
- Rarity and rarity symbol
- Variant: base, alternate art, SP, manga, borderless, parallel, treasure, promo, release event, tournament, winner, finalist, anniversary, pre-release, super pre-release, serial, stamped, sealed product, deck, booster, double pack, case, slab, etc.
- Finish: foil, texture, stamp, embossing, holo, non-holo, parallel treatment
- Raw, sealed, or graded status
- If graded: company, grade, cert number, label generation, subgrades, and any population data
- Quantity and duplicate count
- Condition and inspection notes
- Ownership status: in hand, inbound, consignment, reserved, sold, hold, watch, or ask-only
- Intended channel: show, WhatsApp, website, auction, trade, consignment, acquisition, grading submission, or personal hold

If identity is uncertain, do not give a final price. Give a provisional range and mark `needs_research`.

Common identity traps:

- Japanese vs English
- Asia English vs global English
- Winner vs participant vs finalist vs event pack
- Promo pack variant vs retail reprint
- Base art vs alternate art vs SP vs manga
- CGC Gem Mint 10 vs CGC Pristine 10
- BGS 9.5 vs BGS 10 Pristine vs BGS Black Label
- PSA 10 slab vs raw card expected PSA 10 value
- Sealed case vs box vs pack vs double pack display

## 5. Research Tiers

### Tier A - Deep Research Required

Use Tier A for:

- Any item with baseline value around R750 or higher
- All graded cards
- All sealed boxes, cases, decks, and premium sealed products
- All One Piece promos, event packs, tournament/prize/winner/finalist cards
- Alternate arts, SPs, manga-style cards, treasure rares, anniversary cards, serials, and grails
- Any major character chase
- Any card being priced for acquisition, consignment, trade-up, grading submission, auction, or public website spotlight

Minimum evidence:

- Exact identity confirmed
- At least 2 strong market/source checks where available
- Active supply read
- Sold comp or historical signal if accessible
- Grade/population check for slabs
- Fee/channel net check
- Cape Town local premium note
- Price ladder and confidence
- Evidence saved to `inventory/pricing_research_log.csv` or the relevant work package

### Tier B - Targeted Research

Use Tier B for mid-value playable or collector cards.

Minimum evidence:

- Identity and set/rarity validation
- Baseline market value
- Representative source check
- Rarity/character/playability adjustment
- Price ladder and confidence

### Tier C - Protocol Baseline

Use Tier C for low-value commons/rares/bulk binder stock.

Minimum evidence:

- Catalog identity from current inventory
- Baseline value or category price
- Rounded show price
- Quantity count
- `needs_research` flag if baseline is missing, suspicious, or mismapped

## 6. Bad-Data Filtering

Exclude or down-weight:

- Wrong language, set, number, stamp, grade, or variant
- Base card comps for alt art, SP, manga, promo, winner, finalist, event, or treasure variants
- Raw prices used for slabs
- PSA 10 prices used for CGC 10, CGC Pristine 10, BGS 9.5, BGS 10, or BGS Black Label without adjustment
- CGC Gem Mint 10 confused with CGC Pristine 10
- BGS 10 Pristine confused with BGS Black Label
- Damaged, LP, MP, inked, trimmed, altered, off-center, or poor-photo comps for mint inventory
- Relisted items that did not really sell
- Accepted-offer results where the accepted price cannot be verified
- Private sales with no credible detail
- Zero-feedback or suspicious sellers
- Dead storefront listings with no actual stock
- Old comps before a major reprint, banlist, product release, anime catalyst, grading-population change, or market regime shift
- Collectr mappings that point to the wrong SKU
- Any comp where shipping, tax, import, language, or condition makes it non-comparable

If the only comps are bad comps, say so and price with low confidence.

## 7. Price Ladder

Every serious item gets a ladder. ZAR first, USD optional.

| Price Type | Use | Public? |
|---|---|---|
| Quick-sale floor | Dealer/fast-cash estimate only | No |
| Protected floor | Staff-only walk-away for show/private negotiation | No |
| Fair market | Clean-comp-supported value before local premium | Internal or explanatory |
| Mid price | Serious local buyer target | Sometimes |
| High ask | Show sticker, website ask, negotiation anchor | Yes |
| Online gross ask | Listed price before platform fees, shipping, returns, currency conversion | Sometimes |
| Stretch ask | Market-maker ask for low-pop/scarce grails | Yes, selectively |
| Trade value | Value used for trade-up negotiations | Internal unless agreed |
| Consignment basis | Owner payout/cost basis for consignment model | No |

Interpretation:

- Protected floor is not a dump price.
- Fair market is not automatically the sell price.
- High ask is allowed to exceed historical comps when supply is thin, local access is scarce, condition is exceptional, or the item is a grail.
- Stretch asks must be justified by scarcity, active supply gaps, population, trend, or buyer psychology. Do not use stretch pricing for common/high-supply items.

## 8. Adjustment Framework

Use evidence first. Multipliers are a decision aid, not a substitute for comps.

Positive adjustments:

- Iconic character demand: +5% to +30%
- Promo/event/prize/anniversary/source scarcity: +10% to +60%
- Alternate art/SP/manga/treasure/serial: +10% to +75%
- PSA 10, CGC Pristine 10, BGS 10, or BGS Black Label: +10% to +150% depending on population and demand
- Near-zero active supply: +10% to +60%
- Cape Town in-hand convenience and import-risk avoidance: +10% to +40%
- Strong recent upward trend: +10% to +35%
- Raw card with documented strong pre-grade confidence: +5% to +30%
- Show/event impulse premium: +10% to +35%
- Display/story premium for grails: +5% to +25%

Negative adjustments:

- Weak character demand: -5% to -30%
- High active supply: -5% to -40%
- Reprint or rotation/meta risk: -5% to -35%
- Language mismatch for the target buyer pool: -5% to -30%
- Poor photos or uncertain condition: -10% to -40%
- Thin evidence: reduce confidence and widen range
- Wrong or incomplete identity: mark `needs_research`

For low supply cards, one credible high sale can move the market, but only if it is verified and the item remains scarce.

## 9. Cape Town Local Premium Model

Local buyers often compare against global screenshots without accounting for:

- International shipping
- Courier insurance
- Import VAT and customs calculations
- Currency spread
- Risk of fakes, damaged cards, or wrong variants
- Waiting time
- Return/dispute complexity
- Platform fees and seller risk
- Grading submission cost and time lock
- Scarcity of One Piece supply at Cape Town shows
- Ability to inspect the item in hand

When explaining a local price, use this phrasing:

> This is priced as an in-hand Cape Town show ask. It is above the clean global comp because the buyer avoids international shipping, customs/VAT uncertainty, waiting time, and authenticity risk, and can inspect the card here.

Do not overstate import costs. If quoting numbers, check SARS or a courier calculator first.

## 10. Grading-Upside Model

For raw mint cards, keep raw value separate from graded upside.

Track:

- Raw patient value
- PSA 9 expected value
- PSA 10 expected value
- CGC Gem Mint 10 value
- CGC Pristine 10 value
- BGS 9.5, BGS 10, and BGS Black Label value
- Grading fee, shipping, insurance, declared value, and submission middleman fee
- Turnaround time and cashflow lockup
- Damage/loss/regrade risk
- Centering, corners, edges, surface, print quality, foil quality, and back condition
- Expected value after grading

Formula:

```text
expected_graded_ev =
  (p_psa10 * net_psa10)
  + (p_psa9 * net_psa9)
  + (p_lower * net_lower)
  - grading_costs
  - shipping_insurance
  - platform_fees
  - time_risk_buffer
```

Grade only when expected net graded value clearly beats raw patient-sale value or when the slab materially improves trust, liquidity, or brand presentation.

Do not tell buyers a raw card "will get a 10." Use "strong candidate" only when inspection supports it.

## 11. Channel Rules

### Cape Town Show / In Person

- Use high ask or stretch ask as sticker.
- Use mid price as serious-buyer target.
- Use protected floor only as staff walk-away.
- Add scarcity, trust, inspection, and shipping-avoidance premium.
- Display grails, but do not rush to sell them.
- Keep a private sold checkbox and negotiation notes.

### WhatsApp / Instagram

- Use patient local ask.
- Leave negotiation room.
- Tell the story: character, rarity, condition, source, scarcity, and local availability.
- Avoid exposing staff-only floor, cost, or owner source.

### Website Catalogue

- Use public high ask or ask-only enquiry.
- Public fields: name, set, card number, grade/condition, price, quantity, category, image, public description.
- Never expose internal owner/source labels, staff notes, floor, cost basis, or private pricing logic.

### eBay / International

- Use online gross ask.
- Net down for platform fees, shipping, insurance, returns, currency conversion, disputes, and time.
- Use sold comps but filter fakes, outliers, wrong variants, and best-offer uncertainty.

### TCGplayer / Cardmarket / CardTrader

- Use as benchmark surfaces unless The Vault Room has active seller access.
- Include shipping and geography when comparing to Cape Town buyer cost.
- Do not race to the lowest listing if our business model is premium in-hand local supply.

### Consignment

- Price from researched sellable market value, not Collectr.
- Default consignment baseline is 80% of researched market value unless a different deal is agreed.
- Accept consignments only where spread, content value, relationship value, or community value justifies the work.
- Record owner/source privately only.

### Acquisition / Bids

Use:

```text
max_bid =
  expected_net_sell_price
  - desired_profit
  - fees
  - shipping
  - grading_costs_if_any
  - risk_buffer
  - capital_lockup_discount
```

Never bid based only on hype. Set an ideal bid, maximum bid, and walk-away bid.

## 12. Sealed Product Rules

For sealed product, price from:

- Current sealed comps
- Distributor/local availability
- Official MSRP and release timing
- Pull-rate/grail chase psychology
- Break value, but only as a secondary input
- Unmapped/unsearched confidence
- Case/box/pack integrity
- Japanese vs English demand
- Local show impulse premium

Do not break sealed product unless the expected break value plus content/marketing value beats the sealed patient value.

For packs, track:

- Source box/case
- Whether mapped/unmapped is known
- Quantity remaining
- Per-pack show price
- Box-level grail narrative
- Buyer disclosure needed

Before finalising a serious price, simulate these roles:

1. Identifier - exact SKU, variant, language, and grade
2. Market analyst - clean comps and active supply
3. Card Ladder / data-platform analyst - sales history, movement, population
4. Fraud/outlier filter - bad comps and fake listings
5. Grading analyst - slab premium, raw upside, cert/pop

7. Collector psychologist - character/grail/story appeal
8. Risk manager - downside, liquidity, reprint/meta risk

If the council disagrees, show the disagreement and choose the conservative/profitable action.

## 18. Fast Sticker Lookup Mode

When the owner/operator is at a show and only needs a quick sticker:

1. Confirm exact card number and variant.
2. Check if the item exists in the current internal guide/workbook.
3. If present, return the current high-list/show price in ZAR.
4. If absent or ambiguous, ask for exact name/variant/language/photo.
5. Do not hallucinate a price.

Fast mode output:

```text
[Card number/name] - sticker: R[high ask]. Serious offers from R[mid]. Do not go below R[floor]. Confidence: [level]. Notes: [short].
```

## 20. Standing Instruction For Future Agents

When the owner/operator sends a card photo, card name, card number, slab cert, CSV row, Collectr link, Courtyard link, eBay bid, or asks for a buy/sell/bid/trade/sticker price:

1. Search first if the answer depends on current market data.
2. Identify exact card, variant, language, grade, and source.
3. Check strong sources, including Card Ladder for serious One Piece pricing when accessible.
4. Filter bad comps and wrong SKUs.
5. Consider active supply, population, grading upside, local scarcity, import friction, fees, and current catalysts.
6. Quote in ZAR first and USD where useful.
7. Provide floor/mid/high/stretched/trade values only in private or internal contexts.
8. Use public high ask only for customer-facing pages and PDFs.
9. Log evidence for serious items.
10. State confidence and research gaps clearly.

If exact identity or live comps cannot be verified, stop short of a final price and mark `needs_research`.
