## Negotiation rules I would actually use on the floor

For **HOLD HIGH** cards, start at list and do not move fast. Your first meaningful concession should usually be toward the target price, not below it. If someone wants the BVB Luffy or P-033, make them feel that they are negotiating for access, not for sympathy.

For **LIST HIGH** cards, your real job is to close at target. If a serious buyer bundles two or three premium lines together, you can shave a little off the combined total, but keep the bundle close to your target column. That is where your workbook matters: it stops you from making emotional discounts.

For **SELL NOW** sealed product, I would take target faster. The live markets show too much visible supply for me to treat these as sacred long holds. Best Selection Vol.4 is the one sealed line I would still lean firmer on because the trend is clearly stronger than Live Action and BCG Fest. ţłÇciteţłéturn25search0ţłéturn27search0ţłéturn26view0ţłü

## Open questions and limitations

The workbook assumes each CSV row is a clean sellable unit in the condition represented by the row. If any premium collection products are opened, missing outer packaging, or incomplete, use the **target** or **walk-away** number instead of the **list** number.

The two biggest condition-sensitive lines in the box are **BVB Luffy** and **Magazine Promo Luffy**. If BVB is sealed or especially gradeable, the workbook is conservative. If it has edge wear, use target faster. If the ST21-014 line includes the magazine or sealed presentation, the upside is better than a loose raw card.

This is a strong operating file for shows and private sales, but the top fifteen lines should still be refreshed before any major convention or serious wholesale negotiation, because those lines move the whole business.
